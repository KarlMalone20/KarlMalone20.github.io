--Beihang at a Glance--

Beihang University (previously known as Beijing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics) was founded on October 25, 1952 under the name of Beijing Institute of Aeronautics, originating from the merger of the aeronautical departments of Tsinghua University, Beiyang University, Xiamen University, Sichuan University, etc. Located in the center of the Zhongguancun Science Park and within easy reach of central Beijing, Beihang is the first university in China focusing on aeronautical and astronautical education and research. About 26km away from the main campus, the suburban Shahe campus provides quiet study milieu and is also home to the National Laboratory of Aeronautics and Astronautics (NLAA). The very new Doudian campus aims at research and innovation through engineering combined with medicine.

--The School of Biological Science and Medical Engineering (BSME)--

The School of Biological Science and Medical Engineering (BSME) at Beihang University was established on October 23, 2008, originating from the Department of Bioengineering that was initiated in January 2002. Since its opening, BSME has grown and evolved at a rapid pace and now plays an important role in the development of biomedical studies and education throughout China.

--Teacher Zhang Research group--

Associate researcher Zhang Yongbiao is committed to using multi-omics methods to reveal the etiology of major diseases and high-frequency rare diseases and develop molecular diagnostic markers, and reveal the symmetrical development mechanism of the body based on neural crest cell developmental disorders. He has presided over a number of National Natural Science Foundation of China projects and published more than 20 SCI papers in journals such as Nature Communication and Human Molecular Genetics. The research group mainly uses high-throughput sequencing combined with gene editing technology to explore the pathogenesis of neurological diseases and birth defects. The main research directions include 1) developing new technologies and methods for multi-omics research based on high-throughput sequencing; 2) focusing on major Diseases and high-frequency rare diseases, analyze their pathogenesis, and develop molecular markers that can be used for diagnosis and treatment; 3) Research on the mechanism of symmetrical development of the body based on disease models.
